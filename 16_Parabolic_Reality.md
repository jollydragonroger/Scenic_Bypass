## 16.2 The Parabola in Mathematics

### 16.2.1 Definition

A **parabola** is the set of all points equidistant from a fixed point (the focus) and a fixed line (the directrix).

**Standard Equation:**
```
y = ax² (vertex at origin)
y - k = a(x - h)² (vertex at (h, k))
```

### 16.2.2 Key Properties

**Focus:** The point where parallel rays reflect to converge.
**Directrix:** The line such that distance to focus = distance to directrix.
**Axis:** The line of symmetry through focus and vertex.
**Reflective Property:** Rays parallel to the axis reflect through the focus.

### 16.2.3 Parabolas in Physics

Parabolas appear in:
- Projectile motion (trajectory of thrown objects)
- Reflector dishes (satellite, radio telescopes)
- Suspension bridges (cable shape under uniform load)
- Mirrors (focusing light)

**Common Feature:** Parabolas optimize the relationship between direction and focus.
